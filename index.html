Post-Surgery Knee Rehab: Phased Guidelines

Recovering from a ligament repair or replacement? Learn why phased movement protocols prevent stiff scar tissue and ensure stable joint flex.

Pelvic Floor Activation (Kegels)

Target: Bladder support & core stability
How to Perform:
  1. Sit comfortably, relax your thighs and abdominal muscles.
  2. Squeeze the muscles you would use to stop passing urine.
  3. Hold the contraction for 5 seconds, breathing normally.
  4. Release slowly and rest for 5 seconds.
Sets: 3 Sets Reps: 10 holds Frequency: Twice Daily
Precaution: Do not squeeze your buttocks or hold your breath.

Cat-Cow Spinal Mobility Stretch

Target: Lower back & neck stiffness relief
How to Perform:
  1. Start on your hands and knees (tabletop position).
  2. Inhale: Arch your back down, lifting head and chest. (Cow)
  3. Exhale: Round your spine upwards, tucking your chin. (Cat)
  4. Flow smoothly between both postures matching your breathing.
Sets: 2 Sets Reps: 12 flows Frequency: Morning / Evening
Precaution: Keep movements comfortable; do not over-arch if in pain.

Transversus Abdominis Activation

Target: Diastasis Recti correction & back stability
How to Perform:
  1. Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor.
  2. Place hands on your lower abdomen, near your hip bones.
  3. Gently draw your belly button in towards your spine, bracing.
  4. Maintain a normal breathing pattern; hold for 6 seconds.
Sets: 3 Sets Reps: 10 holds Frequency: Daily
Precaution: Ensure you are not flattening your back excessively.
